§0The premise

Crownfall is the capital the player calls home — the walled city they spawn into, return to between runs, and never have to fight through. Emberfall's crown was stolen and its king went hollow; Crownfall is what the survivors rebuilt in the ash around the empty throne. It is the town half of the loop: everything you do out in the world — story, Crucible, the Waking Depths — is launched from here and settles back here.

✓ Phase 1 is built (2026-07-19)

The walkable shell exists in-engine: all 50 rooms in their fixed layout, correct terrains, the hero spawning on the Crown Plaza, the resident NPCs placed, the three portals wired (Story / Crucible / Depths), and the civic desks opening real menus. It's dev-only for now — reach it from the dev panel → World tab → "▶ Go To Capital". Everything below marked planned (professions, guilds, PvP, daily quests) is still roadmap; the city they live in is now real.

Mechanically it is one fixed-layout zone of 50 rooms (unlike the seeded, procedural spines of the story chapters — see Build Notes). The rooms fall into six rings:

  • The Heart — Crown Plaza, the fountain square you spawn on.
  • The Craftworks Ward — 8 rooms clustered tight around the Plaza: the guild hall, the five crafting professions, the arena sign-up, and the Portal Sanctum. All interconnected, so you circulate between them without leaving the core.
  • The Civic Ring — 7 rooms that give the systems you already have (stash, reforge, records, daily rewards, shop) a physical address.
  • The Approach — 4 rooms: the main gate and the causeway up into the city.
  • The Four Wards — 24 rooms, one district per faction (6 each), where the prominent residents live. Two quest-giver homes per ward = the 8 daily quest-givers, split 2 / 2 / 2 / 2.
  • The Outer Ring — 6 rooms: ramparts, the undercroft, the stables, the physic garden.

Nearly every room reuses a terrain and prop kit that already exists in the build — the environment-seam showcase terrains (ph_forge, ph_kitchen, ph_market, ph_guildhall, ph_garden, ph_fields, ph_library, ph_castle, ph_crypt…). This document is the plan to assemble them into a city, not a request for new art.

§2The Craftworks Ward

Eight rooms in a tight 3×3 cluster with the Crown Plaza at its center — the working heart of Crownfall. Every functional room you asked for lives here, each adjacent to the Plaza and to its neighbours, so a player can loop the whole core in seconds. Five of these front the crafting professions (the profession system is a design seed, not yet built — flagged planned).

The Portal Sanctum — the way out

At the north apex of the cluster, three standing portals are the only doors that leave Crownfall for a run. Each is a built destination — the plumbing exists; the Sanctum just gives it a front door.

§3The Civic Ring

Seven rooms that turn menus you already ship into places. None of these need new systems — they give the vault, the reforge bench, the records book and the daily reward a wall and a keeper, so the hub teaches its own features by being walked.

§4The Approach

Four rooms south of the core — the way in. New players arrive through the Emberward Gate and are funnelled up the causeway to the Plaza, so the first thing they ever see is the city rising toward its fountain and its portals.

§5The Four Wards

One district per faction, six rooms each, in the four corners of the city. Each ward holds two prominent quest-giver homes (marked ●) plus a commons and flavour rooms where the residents live their lives. The two joinable factions — Accord and Cinderborn — hold true wards; the two aloof factions — Wildfang and the Hollow Choir — hold enclaves inside the walls: tolerated, watched, and useful. §7Daily quest rotation

Each of the eight quest-givers offers exactly one quest per day, chosen from a small pre-determined pool unique to that resident. The choice is seeded by the date (plus the NPC id), so every player in a session sees the same board and it resets on a fixed clock — no per-player RNG, no server needed for the single-player build.

How the rotation works

  • Pool per NPC. Each quest-giver owns a fixed list of ~6 quests, authored to their faction's theme and voice. Nothing is generated; the pool is data.
  • Deterministic pick. quest_of_day = pool[ hash(date, npc_id) % pool.size ]. Same day → same quest for everyone; rolls over at the daily reset the game already runs.
  • One turn-in a day. Completing it grants faction standing (the existing faction_standing track), an event currency, and profession mats — then greys out until reset, exactly like the daily-reward desk.
  • Anti-repeat. Optional: exclude the last N days' picks so a giver doesn't repeat within a week, keeping the eight-quest board fresh.

§8The Outer Ring

Six rooms on the wall and under it — the city's edge. Mostly atmosphere and future hooks: the ramparts you can walk, the cistern down into the dark, a stable for mounts that don't exist yet.

§9Build notes

How the hub plugs into what exists, and what has to be written.

Fixed layout vs. the procedural spine

Story chapters build their room graph with a seeded random walk (game_world._generate_layout walks a spine, then attaches side rooms to same-terrain hosts). Crownfall wants the opposite: a hand-authored, stable graph so the guild is always north-west of the fountain and the gate is always due south — muscle memory. The engine's room model is already coordinate-based (each room is a Vector2i cell with per-direction exits), so a fixed hub is an authored coordinate list feeding the same runtime meta the procedural path produces — no new room engine, just a second layout source that skips the walk.

Terrain & prop reuse

Every room maps to a terrain that already ships. The environment-seam work means a room like the Forge is already a working, lit, animated space — drop the player in and the furnace pulses, the flame licks, the fountain shimmers. No room in this plan needs net-new art; it needs authored NPC placement (the ch2 "safe zone" pattern — NPCs at fixed coords with an E — talk prompt) and a handful of new interaction props (a portal, a crafting bench that opens a menu).

Suggested build order

Phase 1 — the shell

A walkable capital

Author the 50-room fixed layout + terrains. Spawn on the Plaza. Wire the three portals and the Civic Ring desks to existing menus. Now the hub is usable as a launcher on day one.

Phase 2 — the living city

Residents & dailies

Place the roster NPCs at fixed coords with dialogue. Ship the daily-quest rotation shell + the eight pools + faction-standing payout.

Phase 3 — the professions

Craft rooms go live

As the profession loop lands, the five craft rooms turn from diorama to workshop — recipes at the Forge, brews at the Alembic, meals in the Kitchen.

Phase 4 — the social layer

Guild & arena

Guild charters in the Chartered Hall; the Proving Gate opens the PvP queue. Fills the reserved co-op plinth in the Sanctum.

§10Open questions

Three calls I made that are worth your eye before anything is coded.

Decisions to confirm

  • The name. You called it Crownfall; the codebase's kingdom is Emberfall (region Vaelscar). I ran with Crownfall as the capital's name — it reads well against Crownless, the city that endured after the crown fell. Say the word if the hub should just be Emberfall (or the rebuilt capital of it).
  • Enemies of the state, living inside the walls. The Wildfang and the Hollow Choir aren't joinable and are semi-adversarial. I framed their wards as enclaves — embassies, tolerated for trade and truce — rather than full districts, so all four factions get their 2/2/2/2 rooms without breaking lore. If you'd rather they be somewhere else (outside the gate, a caravan, a shrine on the wall), that's a small re-map.
  • Scope of "sign-up" rooms. The Chartered Hall (guilds) and Proving Gate (PvP) front systems that don't exist yet. I kept them as real rooms so the city is complete, but they're the two most speculative — fine to build as locked "coming soon" doors in Phase 1.
