# The Scattered Crown

### *An Emberfall Novel — Act One*

---

A companion novel to **Emberfall**, drawn from the lore of Act One and written to stand on its own. It is not a novelization of the game's systems; it is the story underneath them — the people, the choices, and the long argument between a world and the fire it can't put down.

Six hundred years ago the **Concord of Ash** ended the War of Cinders by binding five warring god-kings into the bodies of ordinary mortals. The gods did not die. They grew old and quiet inside their vessels, and the peace was really a held breath. It has begun, at last, to let go.

This is the story of the breath letting go — and of the two people, a generation apart, who carried the world's fire while it did.

---

## Chapters

1. [**The Hollow King**](chapter-01-the-hollow-king.md) — Ser Aldric goes to kill a dead man, and learns what killing him will cost everyone who comes after.
2. [**The Waking**](chapter-02-the-waking.md) — Decades later, a village healer named Elin lays her hands on a sick child, and something answers that she did not call.
3. [**The Unburied Vale**](chapter-03-the-unburied-vale.md) — In a graveyard that has been holding one funeral for sixty years, Elin is asked to commit a mercy that the faithful call murder.
4. [**The Slagfields**](chapter-04-the-slagfields.md) — Beneath the foundries, a court is in session that finds everyone guilty, and the fires have started to answer its sermons.
5. [**The Long Sleep**](chapter-05-the-long-sleep.md) — Under the ice, whole villages are sleeping and refusing to die. To reach the one who gathers them, Elin must first strike a lock she was never meant to break.
6. [**The Blooming Deep**](chapter-06-the-blooming-deep.md) — Where the blight learned to grow instead of rot, a woman cured herself of a curse — and Elin must look at what a cure costs before the same water is offered to her.
7. [**The Breaking Sky**](chapter-07-the-breaking-sky.md) — At the last seal, a man has stopped reciting the words that hold the world together, and started listening to what they were holding back.

## Appendix

- [**The World of Vaelscar**](appendix-the-world.md) — A reader's primer: the Concord, the Crown, the god-kings, the factions, and the Ember that every bearer carries. Light on spoilers; read it first if you like your maps before your journey, or last if you like to find the edges yourself.

---

*Content note: the source material is a game about grief, faith, addiction, and moral compromise. The novel keeps that register — it is often quiet, occasionally very sad, and rarely interested in telling you who was right.*
