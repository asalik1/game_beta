How overrides work: a 32x32 PNG here named after an Art.GEAR_SHAPES
shape (e.g. w_blade.png, icon_sigil.png) replaces the procedural gear
icon, untinted — grade rarity is carried by bag-slot border colors and
item-name colors. potion.png overrides the HUD potion icon (Art.tex).

no third-party terms attached. Generated by the project owner with ChatGPT
(prompts kept in ART_PROMPTS.md at the repo root), then cut to the family spec
by script: background flood-cut from the border, box-downscaled in
premultiplied alpha, hard alpha at 128, quantised to ~12 colours. 32x32,
untinted, zero semi-transparent pixels — same rules as the Raven icons above.
  ward_elixir.png      — cyan flask + shield emblem   -> Elixir of Warding
  renewal_draught.png  — green flask + heart, corked  -> Draught of Renewal

  Re-roll history (2026-07-17): paladin_ult's first render was a UI refresh

  geometry — see ART_PROMPTS.md); warlock_a1 was too dark for the near-black bar.
  assassin_a2 took FOUR: v1 a detailed figure (mush at 32px), v2 dagger + violet
  afterimage ghosts (smeared to a pink streak at menu size), v4 the keeper — a
  bold FLAT shadow silhouette mid-dash with a violet rim. The lesson: a figure
  isn't impossible at icon size, a DETAILED figure is; a flat pictogram reads.
  warrior_a2 (grey-on-grey shield) was reviewed by the owner and KEPT. The set
  is at final quality.

  Two icons were cut down from their renders on purpose: archer_ult and mage_a1
  each came with a ground-impact burst below the subject. Real art, but it
  smears to a grey bar at 24px AND steals pixels from the thing that reads.
