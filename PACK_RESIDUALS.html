Pixel Crawler Residuals — what was not extracted, and exactly why

Companion to ART_QUALITY_REPORT.md and the CREDITS ledger. After the exhaustive two-pass mining of all 11 owned Pixel Crawler packs (~330 sprites extracted, commits 7f45612 and 7ef81c4), this is the complete record of everything left behind. Nothing here was overlooked — every entry is gated on a missing engine capability or was a deliberate judgment call, with the reason and the unlock path recorded so the packs never need re-examination.

Second-source addendum (same day): an owner-prompted recheck of the wider asset library caught two non-Pixel-Crawler packs the "all packs" sweep had skipped — Raven Fantasy Icons and Ninja Adventure. Both are now mined (145 icons + 13 critters extracted); their residuals are recorded in the second-source section below under the same rules.

LANE 1

Ground & floor tilesets

Why the lane is blocked: Crownless paints every floor from procedural palettes in art.gd — terrain identity comes from the ground palette plus macro floor features, tuned against the Forward+ tonemap and the tint value-floor rule in terrains.gd. There is no seam that accepts a seamless ground PNG, so pack floor tiles have nowhere to drop in. Wiring them means a per-terrain ground-override system and re-validating every biome's readability tuning against authored tiles instead of generated ones.

LANE 2

Multi-tile structures & wall decals

Why the lane is blocked: _add_obstacle places exactly one sprite with one circular collider, feet-anchored. Anything that spans tiles, needs rectangular collision, layers onto a wall face, or must connect to its neighbours has no placement path. The unlock is a composite-structure system (multi-sprite footprints + shaped colliders + z-sorting) plus wall-decal support for face-mounted art.

LANE 3

Animated content

Was blocked; now UNLOCKED (2026-07-18): scenery props were static Sprite2Ds — no hframes, no clock. The animated-prop path (hframes + a shared timer, like mob idle strips) shipped in 8b6afc3, and eight strips were cut and wired (bonfire, flame, smoke, forge hearth, grill, pan, sewer + fountain water). The rows below are what stayed behind after that pass — the complex multi-action station grids that don't loop cleanly as a single strip.

SWEEP 2

Second-source packs — Raven Fantasy Icons & Ninja Adventure

Why this section exists: the "all 11 packs" sweep counted only Pixel Crawler — the owner's library also holds Raven Fantasy Icons (~2,190 icons at 16/32/64px; commercial-cleared, no raw redistribution) and Ninja Adventure (CC0; its bosses were tapped once on 2026-07-08 for pc_bosses.gd, the rest never opened). The 2026-07-18 second sweep extracted 145 Raven icons (alchemy draughts, pantry, crafting mats & tools, quest curios, a full gear-icon wardrobe — installed as rv_*, raw crops, no gamma: they live in UI, not the world) and 13 Ninja critters (livestock/wildlife side-strips, pillarboxed + mirrored LEFT + gamma 0.78 — the living-world gap). All of it is placeholder-flagged in content/rv_na_gallery.gd; the codex Future tab gained Alchemy and Critters subtabs. Ninja's Audio/ folder is out of scope here — music/SFX have their own vetted pipeline. What follows is what was left, and why.

Unlock priorities — all three now BUILT

Status update (2026-07-18): the three engine seams that gated Lanes 1–3 shipped in commit 8b6afc3 (Track D), and a showcase pass then wired them with real pack art. What were "priorities" are now demonstrations you can walk into from the dev panel — the terrains named below are all placeholder, so normal play is untouched.

1 · Animated-prop support ✓ built + demonstrated

Scenery props now take the same hframes-plus-clock the mob idle strips use: any prop whose sprite ships a <name>_anim.png strip self-animates. Cut and wired: camp_bonfire, flame, ember_smoke, forge_hearth, cook_grill, cook_pan, sewer_flow and a blue-shifted fountain_flow. Living bonfires, a pulsing forge, a licking hearth, shimmering water — the camps breathe. (Left behind as too-complex-to-loop: the 36-cell anvil, 64-cell sawmill, and 55-cell alchemy grids — multi-action sheets that don't loop as one strip; represented statically instead.)

2 · Composite structures + wall decals ✓ built + demonstrated

Multi-part builds with composite footprints and animated, lit wall decals now place from a Terrains.STRUCTURES catalog. Fourteen authored: ruined gate, watch brazier, old well, signal fire, plus a working guild_forge (animated furnace + flame + smoke + light), cook_hearth, brew_stand, town_fountain, sewer_outfall, great_hearth, market_stall, notice_board, mausoleum, and torch_pillar.

3 · Ground-tile override seam ✓ built + demonstrated

Per-terrain authored floors drop in as ground_<kind>.png (seamless tile or variation grid). Cut: forgefloor, a seamless molten lavafield (used as a lava-channel road), dungeonfloor, hallwood, castletile. No override still means byte-identical shipping biomes; these ride new ground kinds so nothing existing shifts.

Walk them in the dev panel: ph_forge (basalt floor + lava road + working forge), ph_kitchen, ph_dungeon, ph_market, ph_crypt — each fires all three seams at once.

4 · On-demand re-cuts (no feature needed)

Full themed weapon lines, complete crop ladders, A_Hunter combat animations, the anvil/sawmill/alchemy station loops — all sitting in archived, indexed cut sheets. Cut them the day a system asks, in minutes, against real requirements.
